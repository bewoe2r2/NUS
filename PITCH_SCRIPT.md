## 0:00–0:30 — THE HOOK (30s)

**SHOW: /pitch → top of page (Singapore Healthcare Context + Impact Metrics)**

> In Singapore, one in five people over 60 has diabetes. Each one costs the healthcare system two thousand dollars a year. When they end up in the ER, that's eighty-eight hundred dollars per admission. Sixty-one percent of diabetes spending goes to preventable hospitalisations.
>
> Every solution assumes the patient must actively manage their disease. Log your glucose. Remember your pills. Show up for appointments. When they don't, the system blames "non-compliance."
>
> What if the patient didn't have to try at all?

---

## 0:30–1:00 — WHAT BEWO DOES (30s)

**SHOW: /pitch → scroll to "Three Pillars" section**

> Bewo shifts the burden from the patient to the system. Three things make it work.
>
> First, a Hidden Markov Model that gives doctors a 48-hour head start before a crisis hits. It reads 9 biomarkers like glucose and heart rate, most collected passively from wearables.
>
> Second, 18 AI tools powered by Gemini that don't just suggest, they act. Book appointments. Alert caregivers. Adjust nudge timing. All medication changes are doctor-gated.
>
> Third, loss-aversion vouchers grounded in Prospect Theory. Patients start with five dollars a week that shrinks if they disengage. They're not earning rewards, they're fighting to keep what they already have.

---

**SHOW: /pitch → scroll to "As-Is Strategy Canvas" (red ocean radar chart)**

> Here's the current market. Polyclinics and apps like Livongo compete on the same factors with nearly identical value curves. A textbook red ocean.

> We applied the Four Actions Framework. Eliminate manual glucose log reviews, which saves nurse hours. Reduce preventable hospital admissions, which saves eighty-eight hundred dollars each. Raise prediction from zero to 48 hours ahead. And create counterfactual motivation and loss-aversion gamification.

**SHOW: /pitch → scroll to "To-Be Strategy Canvas" (blue ocean radar chart, Bewo overlaid)**

> Bewo's value curve diverges completely. We make the comparison with polyclinics irrelevant. That's value innovation: more value for the patient AND lower cost for the system, simultaneously.

## 1:40–2:50 — PRODUCT DEMO (70s)

**SHOW: /pitch → scroll to "Live Chat Simulation" (animated chat with Mdm. Tan)**

> Mdm. Tan is 72, diabetic, lives with her daughter. Her glucose has been creeping up and her steps are down. The HMM detects she's moving from Stable toward Warning, and Bewo reaches out automatically. She didn't open the app. She didn't press anything.
>
> It detects her mood is low and adjusts its tone. It recommends congee with fish for lower glycemic impact. It awards a voucher bonus for her 6-day medication streak. Then it shows her something no other app can: "Auntie, if you take your Metformin today, your crisis risk drops from 35% to 12%." That's her personalised probability, not a generic reminder.
>
> Behind the scenes, it sends an SBAR report to her nurse, shifts nudges to mornings, and generates a weekly summary.

**SHOW: /pitch → scroll to "Nurse Dashboard" (alert queue + patient cards)**

> The nurse sees Situation, Background, Assessment, Recommendation, a format she already uses. She acts in seconds instead of reviewing glucose logs. And when Mdm. Tan's daughter gets too many alerts, Bewo detects caregiver fatigue and dials them back.

## 2:50–3:50 — COMMERCIAL VIABILITY (60s)

**SHOW: /pitch → scroll to "Three Tiers of Noncustomers" (3-column cards)**

> Our market isn't just existing patients. For tier one, polyclinic patients who disengage because the system is too manual. Bewo requires zero effort. For tier two, pre-diabetics who refuse clinical tools because they don't see themselves as "sick." Bewo feels like a lifestyle app. For tier three, unmonitored elderly managed by family. Bewo's passive sensing covers them with no new behaviour required.

**SHOW: /pitch → scroll to "Price Corridor" then "Revenue Model" then "Market Sizing"**

> B2G model. Government pays, patient pays nothing. One to five dollars per patient per month. At three dollars with forty-cent costs, that's 87% gross margin. One prevented ER admission at eighty-eight hundred dollars pays for twenty-nine hundred patient-months of Bewo.
>
> Singapore is our proving ground with twenty thousand patients across three polyclinics. But the architecture is disease-agnostic. Same HMM, same agentic tools, new emission parameters. ASEAN has fifty-six million diabetics. Globally, five hundred and thirty-seven million. We prove it here, then scale everywhere.
