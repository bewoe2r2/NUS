Bewo

Autonomous AI Health Companion for Type 2 Diabetes
NUS-Synapxe-IMDA Healthcare AI Innovation Challenge 2026 | Problem Statement 1
Mr. Tan is 67, diabetic, lives alone in Toa Payoh. His next clinic visit is 3 months away. Yesterday, his glucose spiked after skipping medication. Nobody noticed — but Bewo did. It detected a dangerous trend in his data, predicted a 78% chance of crisis within 48 hours, messaged him in Singlish he actually understood, checked the message for safety, alerted his daughter via SMS, and sent his nurse a clinical report. He never pressed a button.

The Problem

440,000 Singaporeans live with diabetes — one in nine adults. Between clinic visits (3–6 months apart), they manage alone. The most vulnerable — elderly patients who struggle with phones and forget their medication — are the least likely to seek help. The result: one preventable diabetic ER admission every 6 minutes in Singapore, costing ~$8,800 each (MOH 2024).

What Bewo Does

Bewo monitors patients continuously between clinic visits, predicts health deterioration before it becomes an emergency, and acts — without waiting to be asked.

For patients: Proactive outreach in Singlish. Shows impact of choices — "If you take your Metformin, your crisis risk drops from 78% to 12%." Detects emotional distress through voice check-ins. No digital literacy needed.

For nurses: Urgency-ranked triage dashboard. SBAR clinical reports in 3 seconds (vs 15 minutes by hand). Drug interactions checked across 16 interaction pairs and 45 drug classes.

For caregivers: Three-tier alerts (push/SMS/call). Caregiver fatigue tracking with automatic alert throttling.

How It Works — The Diamond Architecture

Five layers. Safety comes first and last — the AI cannot bypass safety at either end.

Layer 1 checks hard clinical rules before anything else. Glucose <3.0 or >20.0 mmol/L → forced crisis. Drug interactions screened. Patient identifiers stripped before any AI model sees data.

Layer 2 is a Hidden Markov Model that classifies patients as STABLE, WARNING, or CRISIS from 9 health signals. Runs in 12.7ms on a phone, no GPU. Cannot hallucinate — output is pure math, fully traceable. Monte Carlo runs 2,000 futures over 48 hours. Personalises to each patient over time.

Layer 3 — Gemini with 18 healthcare tools decides what to do: remind medication, alert caregiver, generate clinical report, suggest diet change, escalate to nurse. Remembers context across sessions. Reaches out proactively on 6 trigger conditions.

Layer 4 checks every AI message across 6 safety dimensions before delivery. If the classifier fails, the message is blocked — never sent unfiltered.

Layer 5 translates to Singlish via SEA-LION v4 (AI Singapore). Detects emotion from voice via MERaLiON (A*STAR) — catches distress even when patients say "I'm fine."

Does It Work?

Validated on 10,000 synthetic patients (5,000 per round, 32 clinical archetypes). Clean data: 99.3% accuracy, 100% crisis recall. Adversarial data with contradictory signals: 82.1% accuracy, 87.8% crisis recall. Zero patients in crisis were ever classified as safe — in either round. The 12.2% missed in the hardened test were flagged as WARNING (still receiving attention), never as STABLE. 230 tests + 76 validation gates, all passing. +25.3% lift over glucose-only baselines (p<0.0001).

Cost and Performance

Core pipeline: 186ms on consumer hardware, no GPU. Operating cost: $0.40/patient/month. One prevented ER visit ($8,800) funds 22,000 patient-months of monitoring.
