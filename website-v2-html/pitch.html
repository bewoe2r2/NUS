3:00 Commercial Viability — Noncustomers
Three Tiers of Noncustomers

Unlocking demand beyond existing patients

Blue Ocean Strategy identifies three tiers of people who don't currently use chronic disease management solutions.

TIER 1

Soon-to-be Noncustomers

Existing polyclinic patients

Patients who currently visit polyclinics but disengage between visits. They tried the existing system but find it too manual, too impersonal, and too slow.

Bewo's answer: Bewo's zero-effort device integration + proactive AI eliminates the friction that drives disengagement.
TIER 2

Refusing Noncustomers

Pre-diabetics + early-stage

Pre-diabetics and early-stage patients who consciously reject clinical management. They don't see themselves as "sick enough" for a hospital-grade system.

Bewo's answer: Bewo's consumer-friendly chat interface + gamified vouchers make health management feel like a lifestyle app, not a clinical tool.
TIER 3

Unexplored Noncustomers

Unmonitored elderly

Elderly with undiagnosed or unmonitored conditions. They've never been in the market because no one reached them — family caregivers manage informally.

Bewo's answer: Bewo's caregiver dashboard + family alerts + passive sensing means even low-tech-savvy patients can be monitored with minimal effort.

Overcoming adoption barriers with limited resources

RESOURCE HURDLE

Our tactic: Zero hardware cost (uses existing phones + Fitbits). Gemini Flash API at S$0.40/mo COGS. Smart Nation innovation grants cover pilot costs.

Our tactic: Nurses: SBAR reports they already know. Zero new workflow. Patients: zero effort required (passive sensing + AI-initiated). Caregivers: reduces their burden.
